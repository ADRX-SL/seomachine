## Placement rules

- **Primary focus**: Highest and High priority clusters should drive most new content and optimization efforts. These belong in core pages, dedicated articles, and prominent headings (H1/H2) where they fit naturally.
- **Secondary focus**: Medium priority clusters support the money terms. Use them in dedicated articles, subheadings, and body copy to build topical depth and internal linking paths.
- **Supporting focus**: Lower priority clusters are mainly for topical breadth and internal linking. Use them in clusters, comparison content, and sections about sales tooling rather than as primary page targets.
- **One primary per page**: Each major article or page should have a single primary money keyword (or tight cluster variant) in the H1, slug, and meta title. Related variants can appear in H2s and body copy.
- **Natural use only**: Integrate all keywords in natural sentences. Avoid keyword stuffing; aim for helpful, human-first explanations that happen to use the terms.
- **Cross-linking**: Where possible, link from supporting and medium-priority pages back to the highest-priority money pages using descriptive anchor text that includes part of the target keyword.
